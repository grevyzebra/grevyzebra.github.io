Impact of Facebook on personal relationships

According to Debate.Org – 83% of people answered yes to, “Is Facebook actually hurting relationships rather than helping them?” (Debate, 2014)

Prior to the social network era, people developed relationships face-to-face. Face-to-face interaction allowed a person to respond in conversation by applying verbal and non-verbal clues while in face-to-face conversation.

Using Facebook - we cannot convey these messages through Facebook, the only way is interpreting the message with written communication only. However, many people use Facebook as a way to stay in contact, while others use the medium as a way to develop new connections.

A benefit of Facebook or any social websites it allows people to develop or maintain relationships with individuals who may not be close to themselves geographically. When it comes to location, social networking websites allow families, couples, and friends stay connected using a simple click of a button. Facebook has video calling now, therefore making the contact more personal.

What about long term? Marshall, Bejanyan, DiCastro, and Lee (2012) argued that “Facebook claimed to keep people connected but the challenges faced by romantic relationships were unknown” and long term were not successful.

We believe that Facebook does not significantly help or harm relationships. It’s the quality of the relationship is dependent upon the human user. They have the power to who interacts in either a positive or negative way with fellow users through the site. So, the impact of Facebook is seen to be a positive in short terms, but on a longer term basis, it’s more difficult and this is when problems arise.
